Maharashtra Election Results 2024

Maharashtra Election Results 2024 Live Updates: The ruling BJP-led Mahayuti alliance is poised to retain power in Maharashtra, with the latest Election Commission figures showing the alliance leading in 204 out of 288 assembly seats. The opposition Maha Vikas Aghadi (MVA) is trailing, with candidates leading in just 47 seats, according to early trends. TV channels report a landslide victory for Mahayuti, with the alliance ahead in 212 seats and MVA in 68 constituencies as counting began on Saturday morning. As per the official data, BJP candidates are leading in 111 seats, Shiv Sena in 58, and NCP in 35 seats. For MVA, the Nationalist Congress Party (Sharad Pawar) is leading in 9 seats, Congress in 20, and Shiv Sena (Uddhav Balasaheb Thackeray) in 18 seats. Maharashtra Chief Minister Eknath Shinde, along with his deputies Devendra Fadnavis and Ajit Pawar, are leading in their respective constituencies. Shinde is ahead by 4,053 votes in the Kopri-Pachpakhadi seat, Fadnavis leads by 2,246 votes in Nagpur South-West, and Ajit Pawar is ahead by 3,759 votes in Baramati, where he is contesting against his nephew, Yugendra Pawar. In Mumbai, Shiv Sena (UBT) leader Aaditya Thackeray is leading in Worli by 495 votes. Other notable results include Congress president Nana Patole leading by 344 votes in Sakoli, while senior Congress leaders Balasaheb Thorat and Prithviraj Chavan are trailing in Sangamner and Karad South, respectively.The counting of votes began at 8 am on Saturday, with the final turnout for the November 20 election recorded at 66.05%, up from 61.1% in 2019.
